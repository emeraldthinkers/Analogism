# Governance

Analogism is decentralized, but decentralization does not mean the absence of care.

This repository uses a stewardship model designed to protect three things at once:

1. openness to participation;
2. coherence of the movement's central inquiry;
3. transparent public history.

## No Ownership of the Movement

No individual, gallery, company, institution, platform, or repository owns Analogism.

This repository maintains one public version of the manifesto. Other communities may interpret, adapt, translate, fork, or build upon the framework under the terms of the license.

Maintaining this repository does not confer exclusive authority over the movement.

## Maintainers

Maintainers are responsible for:

- reviewing issues and proposed changes;
- preserving a readable and internally coherent manifesto;
- preventing capture by commercial, political, or personal interests;
- documenting significant revisions;
- protecting contributors from harassment;
- maintaining archives, translations, and version records;
- explaining major editorial decisions publicly.

Maintainers are stewards of the repository, not founders with permanent control.

## Decision-Making

Minor corrections may be accepted through ordinary review.

Substantial changes to the manifesto should normally include:

- a public issue;
- a clear rationale;
- a reasonable discussion period;
- a pull request showing the exact proposed language;
- a written summary of the final decision.

Consensus is preferred, but unanimity is not required.

When disagreement remains, maintainers should favor the option that best preserves openness, clarity, plurality, and the central inquiry of Analogism.

## Versioning

The manifesto follows semantic-style versioning as a cultural document:

- **Patch** changes correct spelling, formatting, links, or wording without changing meaning.
- **Minor** changes add, clarify, or substantially revise sections while preserving the movement's foundation.
- **Major** changes alter the movement's foundational definition, scope, or governing principles.

Every public release should preserve its version number and date.

## Forks and Independent Interpretations

Forking is permitted and encouraged when a community wishes to explore a distinct interpretation.

Forks should not falsely represent themselves as the only official form of Analogism. They should preserve attribution and clearly identify their relationship to the source version.

Divergence is not automatically fragmentation. It may be evidence that the framework is alive.

## Conflicts of Interest

Anyone reviewing a proposal should disclose a meaningful financial, institutional, or personal interest that could affect judgment.

No commercial sponsor may purchase editorial authority over the manifesto.

## Removal of Maintainers

Maintainers may lose stewardship privileges for:

- repeated abuse of authority;
- undisclosed conflicts of interest;
- harassment or retaliation;
- deliberate misrepresentation of the repository's history;
- attempts to claim exclusive ownership of Analogism;
- prolonged abandonment without transferring responsibilities.

## Future Governance

As participation grows, this document may evolve toward a broader council, rotating maintainers, public voting, or other transparent systems.

Any future governance structure must remain accountable to the principle that Analogism grows through participation rather than possession.
