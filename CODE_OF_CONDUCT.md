# Code of Conduct

Analogism is an open cultural movement. Participation should expand inquiry, not diminish the dignity or safety of the people involved.

## Our Standard

Participants are expected to:

- engage ideas critically without attacking people;
- welcome artistic, cultural, technical, and philosophical difference;
- give credit where credit is due;
- distinguish disagreement from harassment;
- avoid intimidation, threats, discrimination, stalking, or deliberate humiliation;
- respect privacy and requests for anonymity;
- disclose relevant conflicts of interest;
- participate in good faith.

## Unacceptable Conduct

The following behavior is not acceptable in repository discussions, issues, pull requests, events, or spaces presented as official extensions of this project:

- harassment or discriminatory abuse;
- threats of violence;
- sexual harassment or unwanted sexual attention;
- doxxing or publication of private information;
- plagiarism or deceptive attribution;
- coordinated disruption, spam, or impersonation;
- retaliation against someone who raises a concern;
- attempts to silence disagreement through intimidation.

## Artistic Freedom and Responsibility

Analogism supports difficult, unsettling, provocative, and controversial work.

Artistic freedom does not excuse harassment, fraud, exploitation, or abuse of participants. Criticism of a work, technology, institution, or ideology is not itself a violation of this code.

## Enforcement

Concerns should be reported privately to repository maintainers whenever possible. Reports will be reviewed with attention to context, evidence, privacy, and the safety of those involved.

Possible responses include:

- a private warning;
- removal of a comment or contribution;
- temporary limits on participation;
- permanent removal from repository participation;
- public clarification when necessary to correct material misinformation.

Enforcement decisions should be documented when doing so does not compromise privacy or safety.

## Scope

This code applies to the repository and to spaces, events, publications, or communications that explicitly present themselves as official extensions of this repository.

It does not grant maintainers authority over the entire decentralized movement.
