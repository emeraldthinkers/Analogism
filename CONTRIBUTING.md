# Contributing to Analogism

Analogism is an open cultural framework. Contributions are welcome from artists, writers, designers, musicians, filmmakers, technologists, researchers, craftspeople, curators, educators, and readers.

Participation does not require agreement with every sentence of the manifesto. It does require seriousness, good faith, and a clear connection to the movement's central inquiry: the relationship between technology, art, and human experience.

## Ways to Contribute

You may contribute by:

- proposing revisions to the manifesto;
- writing essays, annotations, or responses;
- translating the manifesto;
- documenting exhibitions, works, or projects associated with Analogism;
- proposing new questions, principles, or definitions;
- identifying ambiguity, contradiction, or unintended exclusion;
- creating tools, archives, publications, or educational resources;
- opening a discussion without proposing an immediate change.

## How Changes Are Proposed

For substantial changes:

1. Open an issue explaining the problem or opportunity.
2. Describe the proposed change and why it strengthens the framework.
3. Allow space for discussion.
4. Submit a pull request with the exact revision.

Small corrections, accessibility improvements, and formatting fixes may be submitted directly by pull request.

## Contribution Standards

A strong contribution should be:

- relevant to Analogism's central inquiry;
- clearly written and understandable outside a specialist audience;
- open to disagreement and revision;
- respectful of cultural, artistic, and intellectual difference;
- transparent about conflicts of interest or institutional affiliation;
- original, properly attributed, and legally shareable.

## What This Repository Will Not Accept

Contributions may be declined when they:

- attempt to establish a single required aesthetic or medium;
- claim exclusive ownership or authority over the movement;
- reduce the movement to promotion for a person, company, product, or platform;
- contain harassment, hate, plagiarism, spam, or deceptive attribution;
- remove openness while offering no compelling reason;
- use the name Analogism without meaningfully engaging its central questions.

## Translations

Translations should preserve meaning rather than imitate sentence structure mechanically.

Create translations under:

```text
translations/<language-code>/MANIFESTO.md
```

Each translation should include:

- the language and locale;
- the version of the source manifesto translated;
- the translator or collective name, unless anonymity is requested;
- a note identifying passages that required interpretive judgment.

No translation is considered more authoritative than the source version from which it was made.

## Attribution

Contributors may be credited in commit history, pull requests, acknowledgments, or translation notes. The manifesto itself remains collectively and intentionally unattributed.

## Editorial Stewardship

Open participation does not mean automatic acceptance. Proposed changes are reviewed to preserve coherence, accessibility, and fidelity to the movement's core inquiry.

Stewardship exists to protect the openness of the framework, not to create ownership over it.

## Conduct

By participating, you agree to follow the repository's `CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md`.
