# Analogism

**An open, evolving contemporary art movement exploring the relationship between technology, art, and the human experience.**

Analogism is not defined by a single aesthetic, medium, or ideology. It is a shared cultural framework for artists, designers, writers, musicians, filmmakers, technologists, researchers, and makers examining how emerging technologies shape perception, creativity, identity, memory, culture, and society.

The movement is intentionally decentralized. It has no governing body, prescribed style, or fixed doctrine. It grows through participation, interpretation, criticism, and contribution.

Within Analogism, no single position on technology is authoritative. Some participants embrace artificial intelligence, robotics, virtual environments, computational media, and emerging technologies as creative partners. Others work through slow, handcrafted, or entirely analog processes to preserve forms of experience increasingly overlooked. Many occupy the territory between these positions.

The dialogue is the movement.

## Repository

This repository is the public home of the Analogism manifesto and its supporting documents. It preserves the movement's development through transparent version history.
