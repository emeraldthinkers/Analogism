# Frequently Asked Questions

## What is Analogism?

Analogism is an open, evolving contemporary art movement exploring the relationship between technology, art, and human experience.

It is a framework for inquiry rather than a prescribed style.

## Is Analogism anti-technology?

No.

Analogism includes artists who embrace emerging technologies, artists who resist them, and artists who work between those positions. The movement is interested in the tension itself.

## Is Analogism only for visual artists?

No.

Writers, musicians, filmmakers, performers, designers, technologists, researchers, craftspeople, educators, and others may participate.

## Does Analogism have a required aesthetic?

No.

A work is not Analogist because it resembles a particular style. It becomes part of the movement through the questions it raises about technology, art, and human experience.

## Who founded Analogism?

The movement is intentionally presented without founder-centered authorship.

Its public framework is collective, open, and designed to grow through participation rather than personality.

## Can anyone call their work Analogist?

Yes, provided the use is made in good faith and meaningfully engages the movement's central inquiry.

The name should not be used merely as decoration or as a false claim of endorsement.

## Is there an official institution or membership body?

No.

There is no governing academy, membership fee, certification process, or exclusive authority over the movement.

## Can galleries, museums, schools, and collectives organize Analogist exhibitions?

Yes.

Independent exhibitions, publications, courses, talks, and projects are encouraged. Organizers should describe their work as an interpretation or presentation of Analogism rather than claiming exclusive control over the movement.

## Can the manifesto be translated?

Yes.

Translations are encouraged and may be contributed through the process described in `CONTRIBUTING.md`.

## Can the manifesto be changed?

Yes.

It is a living document. Changes should be proposed transparently, discussed publicly, and recorded through version history.

## Can the repository be forked?

Yes.

Forks and independent interpretations are allowed under the repository license. They should preserve attribution and clearly identify their relationship to the source version.

## Is artificial intelligence required?

No.

Analogism concerns the wider relationship between technology and human experience. AI is one important part of that inquiry, but not its boundary.

## Are traditional painters and craftspeople part of Analogism?

Yes.

Traditional, handmade, and materially intensive practices may be deeply Analogist when they consciously address attention, memory, embodiment, preservation, labor, or resistance within technological culture.

## Is Analogism a commercial brand?

No.

Organizations and artists may create commercial work connected to the movement, but no company or individual owns the movement itself.

## What does “The dialogue is the movement” mean?

It means Analogism is defined less by a final answer than by sustained engagement with the questions technology creates for art and human life.
